FEMALE OWNED & ALL FEMALE STYLISTS

The Iron Rose Salon is a place where people can come get a cup of coffee, share a laugh, feel at home and all the while get pampered with a new do! We provide the best possible services, products, and experience without all the sh*tty attitude. Our Team is a group of fun loving, creative , professional stylist that make the Iron Rose what it is.

The Iron Rose Salon was created by Oklahoma native, Misty Byrd.

Misty, has had a mad passion for hair since a young age and has been working behind the chair since 2007. Misty loves doing extreme punk rock styles, edgy cuts, and colors. She specializes in color corrections as well as cutting corrections. Single-process color, highlighting of all Techniques, Fantasy hair color, and all the bright colors! Misty uses several different professional color systems designed to achieve each individual’s needs. From highlights to Mohawks! Misty looks forward to helping make all your hair dreams come true.

Although a very busy entrepreneur, in her downtime Misty enjoys riding motorcycles, yoga, digging crystals, her pets, and her family!

Misty's story...

"I feel like one of the lucky ones. Growing up very poor in an abusive home where I was neglected I found myself homeless in the streets with a drug addiction by the age 16.

By 25 I had two boys and was trying to turn it around for them. I found recovery and got clean. Of course, life still happens and I struggled. I had over 30 jobs mostly in fast food. I was on food stamps and DHS assistance. I started as a receptionist in a salon that I loved. The owner encouraged me to go to cosmetology school so I got my GED and went. I worked there several years before I read a booked called "Who Moved My Cheese."

I was inspired to open my own place. I Decided that life is short and I really wanted to do it on my own. I had no idea how to run a business or how hard it would be but I was determined. With just the money I was making behind the chair I decided to open my first salon in 2013 and I had to close 9 months later.

After a year I decided to try it again and borrow some money from a dear friend who has now passed. I reopened the Iron Rose Salon in 2014. It has not been easy, I had no big money, just support of great friends, a loving husband, a dream, and a hellbent attitude! When I re-opened it was just me. Today I have 12 chairs and a receptionist. I have created a space for hairstylist to work where they can be themselves, and not have to conform. They don't have to sell anything or dress a certain way. We listen to loud music and the energy is high! I want people to walk in and feel at home." ~ Misty
